## üé® Core Principles

### 1. Show Process, Not Just Output
Don't just show what you made‚Äîshow **HOW you thought about it** and **WHY you made those decisions**.

### 2. Be Specific
"Reduced cost by 90%" > "Improved efficiency"  
"5 user interviews" > "Conducted research"  
"Changed button color from blue to green because..." > "Optimized design"

### 3. Tell a Story
Problem ‚Üí Research ‚Üí Strategy ‚Üí Design ‚Üí Testing ‚Üí Outcome ‚Üí Reflection

### 4. Be Honest
Share constraints, challenges, what didn't work. It shows maturity and problem-solving.

### 5. Use Visuals
Every section should have relevant images, diagrams, or screenshots. Case studies are visual documents.

## ‚úÖ Quality Checklist

Your case study is ready when it has:

**Content:**
- [ ] Clear problem statement
- [ ] Your specific role
- [ ] Research/discovery process
- [ ] Design/development decisions with reasoning
- [ ] Iterations based on feedback
- [ ] Tangible outcomes or learnings
- [ ] Personal reflection

**Writing:**
- [ ] Active voice ("I created" not "was created")
- [ ] Specific details (numbers, names, quotes)
- [ ] No buzzwords without substance
- [ ] Shows thinking behind decisions
- [ ] Scannable (headers, bullets, short paragraphs)

**Visuals:**
- [ ] High-resolution images
- [ ] Process artifacts (flows, wireframes)
- [ ] Before/after comparisons
- [ ] Mockups in context
- [ ] Real content (no lorem ipsum)

## üí° Pro Tips

### 1. Start with Your Best Work
Don't try to document every project‚Äîfocus on 3-5 case studies that show your range and depth.

### 2. Tailor to Your Audience
Applying to agencies? Emphasize collaboration and process.  
Applying to startups? Emphasize speed and outcomes.  
Applying to enterprises? Emphasize scale and systems thinking.

### 3. Iterate Your Case Studies
Just like you iterate designs, iterate your case studies. Get feedback and improve them over time.

### 4. Don't Wait for Permission
You don't need your client's permission to document your process (though you do need permission to show their final work). Focus on YOUR thinking, not their brand.

### 5. Use Real Project Names
Even for side projects or spec work, give them real names and context. It makes them feel more credible.
