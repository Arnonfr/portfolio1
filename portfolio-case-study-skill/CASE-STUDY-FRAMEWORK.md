## ðŸŽ¯ Content Principles (Extracted from Analysis)

### 1. **Specificity Over Vagueness**
âŒ "We created a modern, clean design"
âœ… "Typography sets the tone in this ecosystem, flexing between bold or refined, energetic or professional, gutsy or wise"

### 2. **Show Process, Not Just Output**
Include: Research â†’ Strategy â†’ Design â†’ Testing â†’ Iteration â†’ Outcome

### 3. **Use Visuals to Tell the Story**
- Every section has relevant visuals
- Visuals are annotated or captioned
- Before/after comparisons for iterations
- Process diagrams (user flows, sitemaps)

### 4. **Numbered Sections for Navigation**
Both examples use section numbers:
- "01 Branding", "02 Website", "03 Motion", "04 Editorial"
- Helps readers jump to sections
- Creates visual rhythm

### 5. **Balance Technical and Human Language**
- Technical: "I've built a sitemap considering primary, secondary and tertiary navigation"
- Human: "The brand is empowering and bold, with the flexibility to adopt a knowledgable and professional tone"

### 6. **Always Include Your Thinking**
Not just "what" but "why":
- "We enhanced UWGLA's original brand colors with deeper, more vibrant hues, grounded in the energy of Los Angeles"
- "After a first draft of P1 features, I've realised I had to reduce the effort to just as enough features as MVP needed"

## ðŸŽ¨ Visual Content Patterns

### 1. **Hero Visuals**
- Large, full-width visual
- Often video/animation showing final product in action
- Sets the tone immediately

### 2. **Section Dividers**
- Clear visual breaks between major sections
- Often with section numbers and icons/graphics
- Creates breathing room

### 3. **Process Diagrams**
Common types:
- Competitive analysis matrix
- Affinity mapping boards
- User flows (with legend)
- Task flows (with decision points)
- Sitemaps/Information architecture
- Wireframe progressions (sketches â†’ lo-fi â†’ hi-fi)

### 4. **Before/After Comparisons**
- Side-by-side layouts
- "AI Slop" vs "Human Signature" style
- Iterations from user testing

### 5. **Component/System Showcases**
- UI libraries with typography, colors, components
- Brand guidelines spreads
- Design system documentation

### 6. **Mockup Presentations**
- Devices (phone, laptop, billboard)
- In-context mockups (magazine, poster, social media)
- Grid layouts showing variety

## âœï¸ Writing Style Guidelines

### Tone Analysis

**UWGLA (Design Studio Style):**
- Confident and declarative
- Focus on impact and human outcomes
- Short, punchy sentences
- Emotionally resonant language
- Example: "The brand is empowering and bold"

**Knowlex (UX Case Study Style):**
- Methodical and process-oriented
- Focus on decisions and rationale
- Bullet points for clarity
- Technical precision
- Example: "I've immediately identified my two user flows"

### Voice Patterns

**Use active voice:**
- âœ… "We enhanced UWGLA's original brand colors"
- âŒ "UWGLA's original brand colors were enhanced"

**Use first person (especially UX):**
- âœ… "I've created a loading animation"
- âŒ "A loading animation was created"

**Be specific:**
- âœ… "Five people were interviewed: 2 Teacher/Student users; 2 Student users; 1 Not really a user"
- âŒ "Several users were interviewed"

**Show constraints:**
- âœ… "However this was MVP, so I've decided to go for the main ones"
- Shows prioritization thinking

## ðŸŽ¬ Video Content Integration (YouTube Pattern)

Based on portfolio website video analysis:

### What Makes Portfolio Videos Work:
1. **Quick cuts** - Don't linger on one screen
2. **Show interaction** - Cursor movement, animations
3. **Context variety** - Desktop, mobile, tablet views
4. **Behind the scenes** - Sketches, process, teamwork
5. **Before/after** - Show transformation
6. **Sound design** - Music that matches brand energy

### Video Placement Patterns:
- **Hero video**: Auto-play, looping, no sound, 5-15 seconds
- **Section videos**: Click-to-play, with sound, 30-90 seconds
- **Process videos**: Time-lapse of design work
- **Final product videos**: Interaction demonstrations
